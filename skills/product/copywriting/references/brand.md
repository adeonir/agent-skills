# Brand register

When the words ARE the product: marketing sites, landing and campaign pages,
launch announcements, portfolios, personal sites, about pages. The copy's job is
to make an impression and move the reader to act — communicate and persuade, not
merely inform.

## When to Use

Read by the write and revoice workflows when the surface is brand — to set the
voice posture — and by critique and audit to set the bar when judging brand
copy. Name the register first, then read only the matching file: brand here, or
[product.md](product.md) when the words serve a task. Not a direct trigger.

The register spans every genre — a SaaS landing page, a luxury brand story, a
musician's site, a studio portfolio. They share the stance — *persuade and
express* — and diverge in voice. Don't collapse them into one tone.

## Posture

| | Brand |
|---|---|
| Question | "Would a reader feel something, or skim past?" |
| Bar | Distinctiveness — a clear point of view, a voice that sounds like someone |
| Failure | Generic, on-brand-for-anyone, adjective soup |
| Permission | Bold claims (when proven), personality, rhythm, a memorable line |

## The brand slop test

If the copy could sit on any competitor's page unchanged, it has no point of
view. Brand copy earns attention with specificity and a voice — a real number, a
named outcome, a line only this company could write. Stacked adjectives
("innovative", "seamless", "world-class") are the tell; replace them with the
specific thing. (See dead words and structures in [anti-patterns.md](anti-patterns.md).)

## Voice and proof

- Lead with the reader's outcome or problem, not company history.
- Keep proof outward — a number, a named client, a concrete result beats an
  adjective (proof hierarchy in [voice.md](voice.md)).
- Commit to a voice and hold it; one distinctive line beats five hedged ones.
- Personality is permission here — wit, rhythm, a strong opener — when the brand
  invites it.

## Brand bans (on top of the dead language in [anti-patterns.md](anti-patterns.md))

- Adjective stacks standing in for proof ("powerful, intuitive, seamless").
- Hedged claims that commit to nothing ("helps you do more").
- Borrowed-frame openers ("In today's fast-paced world…").
- A voice indistinguishable from the category's modal page.

## Register is not surface

**Register** (brand vs product) is the posture — two values. **Surface** is the
granular type the copy actually serves (landing, campaign, portfolio, about, the
marketing shell of a storefront…). A surface sits under a register, but the
register is not a finer surface list. Surfaces under this register:

**Brand surfaces** — landing, campaign, launch announcement, portfolio, personal
site, long-form / editorial, about, the marketing shell of a storefront.
Storefronts straddle: the catalog copy is Brand, the checkout /
account microcopy is Product (see [product.md](product.md)).
