# Copy Frameworks

Headline formulas, content-part types, page shapes, and CTA patterns for writing fresh copy.

## When to Use

Loaded by the write workflow when drafting brand-register copy from intent — headlines, value props, landing and CTA patterns. Apply by the surfaces a project has; these are part patterns, not a project-type gate. Product-register surfaces (dashboards, forms, settings) carry microcopy instead — see [ux-writing.md](ux-writing.md).

## Headline Formulas

Pick by what the copy leads with. Always specific over generic.

**Outcome-focused**
- `{outcome} without {pain}` — "Understand how users experience your site without drowning in numbers"
- `{outcome} by {how}` — "Generate more leads by seeing which companies visit"
- `Turn {input} into {outcome}` — "Turn sales into repeat customers"
- `{outcome} in {timeframe}` — "Get your refund in 10 days"

**Problem-focused**
- `Never {bad event} again` — "Never miss a sales opportunity again"
- `{question naming the pain}` — "Hate chasing invoices?"
- `Stop {pain}. Start {pleasure}.`

**Audience-focused**
- `{thing} for {audience}` — "Advanced analytics for Shopify stores"
- `You don't have to {skill} to {outcome}` — "You don't have to be an SEO pro to rank higher"

**Differentiation-focused**
- `The {category} that {differentiator}` — "The CRM that updates itself"
- `The {opposite-of-usual} way to {outcome}`

**Proof-focused**
- `{number} {people} use {product} to {outcome}` — "50,000 marketers use it to send better emails"

## Content-Part Types

Draft only the parts the surface actually has. Each maps to a `copy.yaml` part.

- **hero** — headline + subheadline + primary CTA; optional social-proof line.
- **social-proof** — logos, a key metric, a star rating, or one specific quote.
- **problem** — name the reader's situation better than they can; hint at the cost of not solving it.
- **solution / benefits** — 3-5 benefits (not 10), each: headline + how + proof if available. Benefits over features.
- **how-it-works** — 3-4 numbered steps; each a simple verb + the outcome.
- **objection / FAQ** — answer the 5-10 real hesitations.
- **final-cta** — recap value, repeat the CTA, add risk reversal.

## Page Shapes

Guidance, not a template to force. Use what the surface needs.

- **Compact** — hero → social-proof → 3 benefits → testimonial → how-it-works → final-CTA. Good when brevity matters.
- **Full** — hero → social-proof → problem → how-it-works → benefits → testimonial → use-cases → comparison → FAQ → final-CTA. Tells a story and handles objections.
- **Enterprise** — adds security/compliance, integrations, role-based use cases, ROI, and a demo/contact CTA.
- **Launch** — hero announcement → demo → highlights → before/after → early proof → offer → CTA with urgency.

A flat list of features is not persuasion — vary the parts so the copy argues.

## CTA Formula

`[action verb] + [what they get] + [qualifier if needed]`

| Weak | Strong |
|---|---|
| Submit, Sign Up, Learn More | Start my free trial |
| Get Started | Get the complete checklist |
| Click Here | See pricing for my team |

## Part Writing Tips

- **Hero** — keep the subheadline to ~20 words, deliberately tighter than the general 1-2 sentence subheadline. If the value proposition does not fit, the value prop is unclear, not the limit too tight — tighten the idea, not the word budget.
- **Problem** — open with recognition ("If you're like most {role}…"), then the specific frustration and its cost.
- **Benefits** — headline = the outcome; body = how, in 1-2 sentences; proof if there is one.
- **How-it-works** — numbered, simple verb, outcome per step ("Connect your tools (2 minutes)").
- **Testimonials** — specific results + role + company beat "Great product!".
