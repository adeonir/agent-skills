# Product register

When the words SERVE the task: app and dashboard UI, forms, settings, data
tables, onboarding, states, navigation — anything where the reader is mid-task
and the copy helps them through it.

## When to Use

Read by the write and revoice workflows when the surface is product — to set the
voice posture — and by critique and audit to set the bar when judging product
copy. Name the register first, then read only the matching file: product here, or
[brand.md](brand.md) when the words are the product. Not a direct trigger.

## Posture

| | Product |
|---|---|
| Question | "Can the reader act without re-reading?" |
| Bar | Clarity — the right word, in plain language, at the right moment |
| Failure | Jargon, ambiguity, cleverness that slows the task |
| Permission | Brevity, consistency, instructional directness; personality saved for moments |

## The product slop test

Not "does it have a voice" — clarity is the point here. The test: can a user
fluent in the category act on this copy without pausing? Product copy's failure
mode is not flatness, it's friction — jargon, vague labels, an error that never
says how to fix it, a button that doesn't name its action.

## Clarity and tone

- Be specific, active, human — "Save changes" over "OK"; tell the reader what to
  do, not only what happened.
- One term per concept, held across the surface (Delete, not Delete / Remove /
  Trash).
- Concise and calm; verbs first, no hype.
- Tone shifts with the moment (success, error, loading) on top of a constant
  voice ([voice.md](voice.md)).

## Product bans (on top of the dead language in [anti-patterns.md](anti-patterns.md))

- Jargon or internal labels where plain user language belongs.
- Bare error codes or "Something went wrong" with no next step.
- Placeholder-only fields (the label vanishes the moment the reader types).
- Cleverness or humor in an error — the reader is already stuck.

## Register is not surface

**Register** (brand vs product) is the posture — two values. **Surface** is the
granular type the copy actually serves (dashboard, settings, form, data-table,
onboarding, empty-state…). A surface sits under a register, but the register is
not a finer surface list. Surfaces under this register:

**Product surfaces** — dashboard, settings, form / wizard, data-table,
onboarding, empty-state, navigation, checkout / account, authenticated tools.
Storefronts straddle: the checkout / account microcopy is Product, the catalog
copy is Brand (see [brand.md](brand.md)).
